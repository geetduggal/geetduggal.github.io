## The Pocket Bullet Journal

There are plenty of pocket notebooks out there: Field Notes, Leuchtturm pocket editions, Moleskine cahiers. They work well as single purpose notebooks, but otherwise they tend to become either scrap repositories (loose, unconnected jottings with no through-line) or single-purpose artifacts, like a grocery list or a meeting scratch pad.

What Ryder Carroll‚Äôs Pocket Bullet Journal does differently is bring structure to pocket note-taking without adding burden. That structure matters. It‚Äôs the difference between a pocket full of scraps and a daily log that actually belongs to a larger system.

## One Page, One Day (Roughly)

The practice I‚Äôve settled into is roughly one page per day, anchored by a daily highlight.

Each day, I set a single intention: the one thing I want to accomplish or focus on. At the end of the day, I reflect briefly (sometimes just thirty seconds) on what actually stood out, whether or not it matched my intention. A few words. No full sentences, no overthinking.
