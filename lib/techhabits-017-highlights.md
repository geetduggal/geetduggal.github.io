## Daily Highlights as All Day Events

The idea of a daily highlight isn‚Äôt new, but I‚Äôve found that the implementation matters enormously. For me, creating the daily highlight as an all day event in my calendar has been the most effective approach.

Why all day events specifically?

**First, visibility.**  When you open your calendar, all day events sit at the top. They‚Äôre not buried in the timeline of meetings and appointments. Your highlight is the first thing you see when you look at your day.

**Second, they don‚Äôt compete with time blocks.**  A highlight isn‚Äôt necessarily something you do at 9am or 2pm. It‚Äôs the through line of the day, the thing you want to be true about today when you look back on it. All day events capture that intention without forcing you to schedule it into a specific slot.

**Third, reflection becomes effortless.**  I recently went through the past few months of my calendar, and I was struck by how much useful information I could quickly collect from my all day event highlights. What was I focused on in October? I could see it at a glance. Which weeks felt scattered versus intentional? The days without highlights stood out as gaps.

The calendar becomes a kind of log, but one that‚Äôs immediately visible when you‚Äôre planning or reflecting. You don‚Äôt have to open a separate journal or notes app.  [The story of your days is already embedded in the tool you use to plan them](https://medium.com/@geetduggal/have-you-been-using-your-calendar-all-wrong-9e686de42237).

I generally regret the days I didn‚Äôt get around to creating an all day event highlight. Those days feel like gaps in my own story. I‚Äôm looking to be more consistent about this going forward.

## Multiscale Planning with Year Calendars

Through Nick Milo, I came across the concept of a ‚Äúyear calendar‚Äù via  [Bird‚Äôs Eye Cal](https://birdseyecal.com/). It‚Äôs a simple idea: a single view of an entire year, with each day as a small cell.

You can zoom out and see patterns, seasons, and rhythms rather than just the next week or month. You can ask: what do I want the next quarter to be about? And then you can see whether your daily highlights are actually adding up to that.

This is multiscale planning in practice. The daily highlight connects to the weekly rhythm, which connects to the seasonal arc, which connects to the yearly story. And the all day event is the atomic unit that makes all of it visible.
