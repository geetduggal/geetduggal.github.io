I've been writing about the [Plain Text Habits Suite](https://github.com/geetduggal/geetduggal.github.io/blob/master/tools-readme.md): a trinity of complementary tools grounded in the principle of "powerful user experience on top, simple and readable plain text underneath."

The first two components are Obsidian plugins — Kanban Plus and Full Calendar Plus (installable via [BRAT](https://github.com/TfTHacker/obsidian42-brat)) — described as "vibe-coded into existence, AI-assisted but not AI-dependent."

## The Third Tool: Thousand Words

The newest component, "Thousand Words," differs fundamentally. It's "AI-first in a more load-bearing sense" and addresses a specific challenge: meaningful work happening in messaging apps like WhatsApp, Slack, and Messages becomes functionally ephemeral despite technically existing.

Thousand Words captures screenshots of conversations and uses AI to propose targeted updates to existing notes about people, projects, and spaces. The workflow prioritizes human oversight — the agent "analyzes, suggests, and then waits" for review before any changes take effect.

The tool follows the "[Superpowers](https://github.com/obra/superpowers/blob/main/skills/brainstorming/SKILL.md#process-flow) pattern," functioning as markdown skill files "symlinked into your agent's skill directory" and compatible with Claude Code, Cursor, and similar platforms.

## Additional Experiments

Two previous experiments get an update:

1. **Johnny Decimal modification:** Flattening and removing decimal structure while preserving organizational essence has proven successful, though standard file browser navigation is occasionally missed.

2. **Pocket Bullet Journal and Daily Highlights:** Both digital and physical implementations prove useful across multiple timescales, with natural endpoints occurring at 1–3 day intervals for highlights.
