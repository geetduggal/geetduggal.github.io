More often than not these days, I‚Äôve been revisiting certain books, movies, and articles I‚Äôve enjoyed way back in the past. I‚Äôm always surprised at how much I forget. ## 3. Use your Voice to Take Notes and Journal

I often find it easiest to make progress on abstract topics while I walk. This ‚Äòwalk-and-talk‚Äô model provides a rhythm to my thinking that always seems to result in a satisfying concrete end result both physically and mentally.

There are two types of ‚Äòwalk-and-talk‚Äô habits I perform daily:

2.  Voice journal using a simple voice memo app. I‚Äôve been using Apple‚Äôs ‚ÄòVoice Memos‚Äô app for ages. While I‚Äôve tried others and I like them (eg. Otter.ai), this app is free and supports long-form recording on my phone, Mac, and watch. Of all my digital habits, this one centers me the most and brings me the greatest peace and energy moving forward.

At the end of my recording or note taking, I will often process my thinking using AI tools and transcript as a note in a  [notable folder](https://medium.com/@geetduggal/this-one-idea-changed-the-way-i-organized-my-notes-forever-f293371a0d5b).

I may write more about this in the future, but I can‚Äôt overstate the power and peace of mind that comes from deliberately creating my own plan, rather than having one handed to me by people or apps.

## 6. Sculpt Digital Thoughts into Analog Truths

When I think about analog mediums: raw voice recordings, notes on a piece of paper, etc, I tend to think of capturing information in its rawest form or reading it in some final printed form.

Lately, I have found that after a lot of raw thoughts get curated and refined through my digital workflow, I like to open up  [my Bujo](https://medium.com/@geetduggal/atlas-journaling-a-unified-approach-for-seeing-the-big-picture-and-acting-with-focus-78804f26f5ab)  and write down conclusions, truths, and information I‚Äôd like to more deeply connect with. Physical writing and audio/video recording is one of the best ways to do that IMHO.

This approach avoids the ‚Äòstaring at a blank page‚Äô problem since I already have something I want to write about. It also addresses an issue I think many of us have with notebooks: we don‚Äôt want to ruin them with our messy chicken scratch. If the content going into them is already refined then it‚Äôs more a matter of transcription than invention.
