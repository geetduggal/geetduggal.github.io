I’ve been a subscriber to a service called Readwise for quite a while now and I feel it is truly game changing.

What Readwise lets you do is capture your highlights and notes from many different sources (e.g. Kindle, Instapaper, Pocket, Shortform, …). The overall idea is that highlighting or capturing notes from what you read is pretty useless unless you revisit them, and most people probably don’t.

Readwise offers a way to review your highlights via Spaced Repetition to remember useful highlights and incorporate them into your work. Using their service to periodically review the highlights using this method can prove invaluable.

For example, if I come across a resurfaced highlight that resonated with me in my “Readwise review’, the nice thing is that I’ve set up Readwise to export my highlights to Obsidian where the article is already represented as a note. I find that this is really useful to help better isolate useful ideas and connect them with my other notes and what’s on my mind.
